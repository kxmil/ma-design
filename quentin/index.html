QUENTIN

My initial interest is to look into how machines can help us form stronger connections to nature. I came to Iceland thinking that working around this subject with volcanoes is just the best idea, but

I have come to realize that people already have a very strong connection to them. That’s why I wanted to shift my focus a little bit. Now I’m very interested in nature and plants in the city that get easily ignored.

The function of the machine is to reveal what kind of phenomena are happening inside the plants. For example, plants live on a very different time scale than us humans and they move really slowly, especially here in Iceland. I want to make the movement and life in the plants visible for humans to strengthen our connection.

I’m also thinking about how to make this relationship into an interaction. To consider what humans give in this relationship, not just what we get. The function of the machine could also be helpful only for plants and not for humans. I am looking for how the plants can be more than just a passive participant.

In this project I am prototyping three processes: movement, sound and transaction, and also mixing them. If we could hear the sound plants make, would we relate to them more?
